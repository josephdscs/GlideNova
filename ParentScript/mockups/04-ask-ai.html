Child's age: 3 years

My 3-year-old won't get dressed in the morning. Every single day it turns into a power struggle — screaming, refusing, throwing clothes. We're always late.

Get Scripts
AI Response

Why it's happening at age 3

At 3, autonomy is the developmental priority. Getting dressed is one of the few areas where they have real control — which makes it a likely battleground. The morning rush adds sensory and time pressure that overwhelms their regulation capacity.

Say this (3 scripts)

"Do you want to put on the shirt first or the pants first?" (offer a choice within the non-negotiable)
"You get to pick — the blue shirt or the striped one. Your call." (frame it as their decision)
"We're leaving in 5 minutes. If clothes aren't on, we go in pajamas. No big deal." (natural consequence, said warmly)
